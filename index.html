Key Findings

What the data reveals

Five findings emerged from the exploratory analysis and predictive modeling of real PetNet platform data.

FINDING 01
Followers is the primary engagement driver
Audience size has the strongest correlation (0.73). Creators with 6k-8k followers average 480 engagement — more than double those under 1k (222). Distribution scale outweighs content quality at this stage.
Impact: High
FINDING 02
Comments signal deep engagement
Comment count is the 2nd strongest predictor (0.60). High comment volumes indicate active participation, not passive scrolling — a high-quality signal for the platform algorithm to prioritize.
Impact: High
FINDING 03
Video outperforms photo by +9%
Video posts average 388 vs. 356 for photos. 58.5% of posts are already video. Duration also matters: videos between 75-90 seconds show the highest average engagement across all buckets.
Impact: Medium
FINDING 04
Engagement follows a Pareto pattern
The top 20% of posts generate approximately 80% of total platform engagement. A minority of high-follower creators drive the majority of interaction — a concentration risk and a growth opportunity.
Impact: Medium
FINDING 05
Posting time has near-zero influence
Correlation between post_hour and engagement is -0.075 — essentially noise. The algorithm distributes content regardless of publication time. Optimizing posting time is a low-ROI strategy for PetNet creators.
Impact: Low
FINDING 06
Hashtags have minimal algorithmic weight
Hashtag count correlation is 0.039 — near zero. Unlike Instagram or TikTok, PetNet's algorithm doesn't appear to use hashtags as a ranking signal. Creators should not invest in hashtag research.
Impact: Low
